# Dataspace Participation Agreement — Independent Controller

Version 1.0 — effective 2026-09-01

This agreement governs an organisation's participation in the dataspace where
it determines its **own** purposes and means of processing.

## 1. Capacity

The participating organisation acts as an **independent controller**. It is not
processing on another controller's documented instructions: it decides why the
data is processed and is separately accountable for that decision.

Consent under GDPR Art. 4(11) is consent to a specific controller's processing.
An organisation holding this agreement is therefore **outside** the consent
circle of any sharing offer whose controller is a different party — its access
requires a consent decision of its own, addressed to the data subject and
naming this organisation.

## 2. Obligations

- Establish and document a lawful basis for every processing operation it
  performs, independently of the disclosing controller.
- Provide its own transparency information to data subjects under Art. 13/14.
- Process personal data only for the purposes consented to it by name.
- Maintain appropriate technical and organisational security measures.
- Notify the dataspace operator without undue delay of any personal-data breach.

## 3. What this agreement does not grant

Acceptance of this agreement is **not** admission to another controller's
processor category. It does not permit processing under a disclosure made to
that controller's data subjects, and it does not shorten or substitute for the
consent request that Art. 4(11) requires.

## 4. Credentials and revocation

Participation is evidenced by an OrganizationCredential issued by the dataspace
trust anchor. Suspension or revocation of that credential withdraws the
organisation's authority to transact, and the revocation is published through
the dataspace StatusList.
