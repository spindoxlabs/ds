# 07 — Governance Interoperability

## Overview

Data space governance addresses regulatory dynamics, decision-making authority, stakeholder participation, and accountability. In the energy domain, new paradigms (DER, e-mobility, flexibility) create unprecedented stakeholder interactions defined by the HEMRM.

## Governance Framework Layers

Four distinct layers (per OPEN DEI):

| Layer | Scope |
|---|---|
| **Common European framework** | Private-public data governance (Data Act, Data Innovation Board) |
| **Domain-specific building blocks governance** | Inter-data spaces governance |
| **Data space governance** | Intra-data space governance |
| **Governance of soft infrastructure** | Operational level — essential services provision |

## SGAM Mapping and the 6th Layer Proposal

The governance interoperability components are mapped to:
- **Local side** (distributed data ecosystems with legacy data platforms)
- **Federated side** (federated data space)
- **Five SGAM interoperability layers** (vertically)

Ten building blocks are defined across every SGAM layer for governance interoperability.

### The 6th SGAM Layer: "Framework"

The int:net whitebook proposes adding a 6th SGAM layer called "Framework" because the existing 5th layer (business) cannot cover political, regulatory, or societal interoperability.

**Stakeholders addressed by the Framework layer**:
- Policymakers (national to municipal)
- Regulatory bodies
- Market operators (global to local)
- Standardisation organisations
- Supplier associations (ENTSO-E, DSO Entity, T&D Europe, AIOTI, SmartEn, SolarPower Europe)
- Consumption associations (industry, building, consumer)
- Research, innovation, and funding programmes
- Education and human capital institutions
- Infrastructure operators (transport, health)
- Finance and investment institutions (ECB, EIB, EFRAG)

This layer enables identification of interoperability barriers and requirements previously hidden, enhancing governance fulfilment.

## Governance Building Blocks (per DSSC Blueprint v1.0)

### Organisational Governance
Key decision points:
- Scope of the data space
- Position in the ecosystem
- Openness concerning new participants
- Support arrangements for participants
- Principles (e.g., democratic governance)

Goal: Promote collaborative, multi-stakeholder governance.

### Data Sharing Governance
- Rules and standards for security, performance, interoperability, and observability of data transactions
- Mandated by the governance authority as part of the governance framework
- Essential for building trust between participants

## Governance Model Requirements

The governance framework must:
- Acknowledge diversity of platforms and systems tailored to various market designs
- Promote cross-stakeholder, cross-border, and cross-sector data exchanges
- Guarantee convenient data access compliant with GDPR
- Facilitate TSO-DSO coordination from customer perspective
- Ensure scalability through open-interoperable principles
- Leverage common open-source components and agreed-upon rules

## Differences from DSSC Governance Approach

| Aspect | DSSC Blueprint v3.0 | CEEDS Blueprint v2.0 |
|---|---|---|
| **Governance scope** | Rulebook, trust framework, participation rules | Same + SGAM-mapped, energy-regulatory-aware |
| **Layer model** | Three conceptual layers (Governance / Identity & Policy / Data) | Five dimensions + four governance layers + proposed 6th SGAM layer |
| **Domain specificity** | Domain-agnostic | Energy-specific (HEMRM, SGAM, grid codes, demand response codes) |
| **Regulatory depth** | GDPR, DGA, Data Act, eIDAS, AI Act (generic) | Same + energy-specific directives (Directive 2019/944 Art. 23, demand side flexibility code, ENTSO-E/DSO Entity network codes) |
| **Stakeholder mapping** | Generic (governance authority, participants, service providers) | Detailed energy actors (TSO, DSO, FSP, CPO, prosumers, etc.) per HEMRM |
| **Cross-sector** | Mentioned as goal | Explicitly modelled (energy ↔ mobility, energy ↔ buildings) |
